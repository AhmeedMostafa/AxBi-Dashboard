# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Business users, analysts, and students who upload CSV/XLSX business data (Sales, Marketing,
Operations, HR) and need insight without writing code. Their context: sitting at a desk, often
non-technical, wanting answers fast — "what happened, what's coming, what should I do." They work
across upload → auto-dashboard → forecast → narrative report → recommendations.

## Product Purpose

AxBi turns a raw spreadsheet into an interactive dashboard, AI forecast (model competition),
narrative business report, and segmentation — automatically, via an 8-step async pipeline. Success
= a user drops a file and within minutes trusts the charts, the forecast, and the recommended
actions enough to act on them.

## Brand Personality

Modern, friendly, approachable. Confident but not intimidating — it lowers the barrier for
non-analysts while still reading as a credible analytics tool. Voice: plain, active, helpful;
explains numbers in human terms; never jargon-for-jargon's-sake.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS template** — no gradient-blob hero, no endless identical icon+heading+text card
  grids, no purple-on-white clichés.
- **Cluttered legacy BI** (old Power BI / SAP look) — no dense grey enterprise clutter, no tiny
  cramped controls, no wall-of-data with zero breathing room.

## Design Principles

1. **Explain, don't just display.** Every chart/forecast carries a plain-language "why" — the
   product already attaches reasons and grain; the UI must surface them, not bury them.
2. **Calm density.** Show a lot of data without feeling cluttered — generous spacing, clear
   hierarchy, one focal point per view.
3. **Trust through clarity.** Readable contrast, honest states (loading/empty/error), no hype.
   Credibility beats flash.
4. **Approachable defaults, expert depth.** Sensible defaults for newcomers; advanced controls
   (model picks, grain, features) available but not in the way.
5. **One consistent system.** A single token set for color/spacing/radius across every page —
   light by default, dark on demand — no per-page divergence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, visible keyboard focus, `prefers-reduced-motion`
honored. Color is never the only signal (pair with icon/label/text). Both light and dark themes
meet AA.
